01

The idea

What the brand has to say before anyone reads a single doc. Warm, plainspoken, and hopeful about what people can grow with it.

Wire up a tool, hand it a memory, let it draw its own screen — and an agent starts doing things that used to take a whole team.

Use any tool

Reach for any API or MCP server and put it to work like it was always there.

Remember

Hold onto what happened and what’s true — not just the last few messages.

Render UI

Draw a live interface straight from a tool call, so the work shows up instead of hiding in text.

Reason

Compose every step over one calm surface instead of a maze of bespoke wiring.

  • Keep the glove icon on its electric-blue field — the blue is part of the mark.
  • Set the human wordmark in Fraunces, lowercase, always “glove”.

✕ Don’t

  • Don’t capitalize (“Glove”) or stylize it (“gLove”, “GLOVE”).

THE SUN RULE → one accent per surface. On daylight, keep bright Sun for fills, underlines and the disc; switch to Deep Amber for any accent that’s text. On dusk, Sun is your text accent. Never two accents fighting on one screen.
The same brand at dusk — product, docs, terminals. Cream lacing becomes the text, the sun stays the accent, and the sky-blue does the linking. Day and night are one system, not two brands.

05

Motif & texture

The signature is a sunrise over a cultivated horizon — the Cradle sun rising into daylight. Structure is rectilinear; only living things and the sun are allowed to curve.

The sun, rising

Cradle Research’s sun-over-water mark is the seed of the whole system. glove inherits it and lets it break into daylight: a gold disc, a bright horizon, and the reflection bands thinning downward into water.

“Straight edges for structure. Curves only for living things and the sun.”

06

Voice & tone

Solarpunk optimism, engineer’s precision. Invite people in on the landing page; get exact the moment they open a doc.

Inviting, not technical

Lead with what someone can grow or do. Save the schema for the docs — the front door is for possibility.

Plain verbs, real nouns

Hold, remember, see, reason. Name things people recognize, never how the system is built.

Hopeful, never hype

Confident about what’s possible without overselling. Warmth is earned by being specific.

Lowercase, unhurried

The wordmark is lowercase; the tone matches. Calm, human, sentence case — like sunlight, not a siren.
